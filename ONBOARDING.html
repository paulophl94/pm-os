Step 1

What is Cursor?

Cursor is an AI-native editor — think of it as a text editor with a built-in AI assistant. It reads, creates, and edits files in your workspace. You don't need to know how to code. Just open the chat panel and describe what you need in plain language.

    1

    Chat Panel — Your main interface

    Press ⌘L (Mac) or Ctrl+L (Windows) to open it. This is where you talk to PM OS. Type what you need in plain language — no special syntax required.

    2

    Agent Mode — AI that acts

    Unlike a simple chatbot, PM OS can read and create files in your workspace. It loads your product context, understands your strategy, and generates real documents you own.

    3

    Rules & Skills — The brain

    Rules are persistent instructions that shape every response (templates, tone, coaching). Skills are multi-step workflows (daily briefing, meeting processing). Both live in .cursor/ and you can customize them.

    ⚡ Quick Start
    1. Download Cursor and install it
    2. Clone the repo: git clone https://github.com/paulophl94/pm-os.git
    3. Open the pm-os folder in Cursor
    4. Press ⌘L to open the Chat Panel
    5. Type: /start — the wizard will ask you to pick a setup tier (Quick 5 min, Full 15 min, or Power 25 min) and guide you from there
    Step 4

    The Toolkit

    14 document templates, 6 review personas, 8 workflow skills, and 6 strategy frameworks — all context-aware. No commands to memorize — just describe what you need in natural language.

    🎯 Structured requests

    Create a document
    "Create a PRD for the new onboarding flow"
    Get feedback
    "Review this as an engineer"
    Start your day
    "Morning briefing" or "What's on today?"
    Process a meeting
    "I had a meeting about X, here are the notes"
    Check tasks
    "What's pending?" or "Show my to-dos"

    💬 Casual and multilingual — works too

    Portuguese
    "Criar PRD para onboarding" / "Gerar One-Pager"
    Casual
    "What would an engineer think of this?"
    Casual
    "Good morning" / "Começar o dia"
    With context
    "Process notes from my sync with the design team"
    Casual
    "O que tenho pra fazer?" / "Meus to-do's"
    💡
    Pro Tip: Be Specific
    Good: "Create PRD for self-service password reset on mobile"
    Vague: "Create PRD for authentication"

    The more context you give, the better the output. Include the scope, platform, and user segment whenever possible.

    Setup Checklist

    Track what's configured in your workspace. The /start wizard handles the essentials — come back here to see what you can fill in over time.

    🎯
    Product Context
    • ☐ Product Identity (name, model, differentiator)
    • ☐ Active Domains (missions, statuses)
    • ☐ Working Preferences (language, tone, tools)
    • ☐ Terminology glossary
    File: .cursor/rules/product-context.mdc
    🧭
    Strategic Foundation
    • ☐ Strategic pillars (2-4 themes)
    • ☐ Quarter goals (3-5 outcomes)
    • ☐ Week priorities (Top 3)
    • ☐ Strategic assumptions
    Folder: global-context/shared/ & to_do's/
    🌐
    Domain Context
    • ☐ Domain vision & strategy
    • ☐ Domain KPIs & targets
    • ☐ Current roadmap
    • ☐ Architecture overview
    Folder: global-context/{domain}/
    👥
    People & Users
    • ☐ Key stakeholder person pages
    • ☐ User personas
    • ☐ Commitment tracking started
    Folder: context/people/
