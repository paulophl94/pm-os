# PRD: Subscription Pause Feature

**Author:** Example PM
**Date:** 2026-01-15
**Status:** Draft

---

## 1. Market Problem

Subscription-based e-commerce stores face a significant retention challenge: when subscribers experience temporary financial constraints, lifestyle changes, or product surplus, their only option is cancellation. This binary choice — stay or leave — forces a permanent decision during what is often a temporary situation.

The **market opportunity** is substantial. The subscription e-commerce market is projected to reach $904B by 2026. With average churn rates of 6-8% monthly, even a modest reduction in involuntary churn represents significant retained revenue. Industry benchmarks show that pause functionality can reduce cancellation rates by 15-25%.

## 2. Why Solve This?

1. **Retention lever:** Subscribers who pause are 3x more likely to resume than those who cancel and need to be re-acquired.
2. **Revenue protection:** Each prevented cancellation preserves the full LTV of that subscriber relationship.

This directly increases business value by reducing acquisition costs (retained subscribers don't need re-acquisition campaigns) and improving predictability of recurring revenue forecasts.

## 3. Competition Summary

- **Shopify Subscriptions** — offers native pause with configurable duration limits
- **ReCharge** — full pause/skip/swap flexibility, market leader in subscription management
- **Bold Subscriptions** — skip-next-delivery model, less flexible than full pause
- **Ordergroove** — enterprise-grade pause with automated resume campaigns
- **Chargebee** — billing-level pause with webhook integrations

## 4. Target Customers

The customer is the **store owner** who sells subscription products.

- Customers want to reduce churn without giving permanent discounts
- They prefer our product because of native platform integration (no third-party app)
- Their buying decision is influenced by: feature completeness vs competitors, ease of setup, and impact on their subscribers' experience
- Their goal is to maintain subscriber relationships through temporary disruptions

## 5. Success Criteria

Reduce subscription cancellation rate by 15-20% within 90 days of launch. Achieve 40%+ adoption among stores with active subscriptions. Maintain NPS score above current baseline (no degradation from feature complexity).

## 6. Feature List

1. Subscriber-initiated pause with configurable maximum duration
2. Store-configurable pause rules (max pauses per year, minimum subscription age)
3. Automated resume with pre-notification emails (3 days before)
4. Admin dashboard showing paused subscriptions and projected resume dates
5. Pause analytics (pause rate, resume rate, pause-to-cancel conversion)

## 7. Product Requirements

### Development Requirements
The feature requires a new subscription state ("paused") in the existing state machine, modifications to the billing engine to skip charge attempts during pause periods, and API endpoints for pause/resume operations compatible with the existing subscription API.

### Compatibility Requirements
Must work across all storefront themes without custom code, support both credit card and payment link billing methods, and maintain backward compatibility with existing subscription API consumers.

### Performance Requirements
Pause/resume operations must complete in under 500ms, the billing engine must efficiently skip paused subscriptions without performance degradation at scale (10K+ paused subscriptions), and resume notifications must be delivered with 99.5% reliability.
