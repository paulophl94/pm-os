# One-Pager: Subscription Pause Feature

**Author:** Example PM
**Date:** 2026-01-12
**Status:** Approved

---

## TL;DR

Allow subscribers to pause their subscription temporarily instead of canceling. Affects ~2,000 subscriptions/month (30% of cancels). Estimated to reduce churn by 15-20% with medium technical effort (2 sprints). Positive ROI in 3 months.

---

## Problem

### What is happening?
When subscribers face temporary situations (financial constraints, product surplus, travel), their only option is full cancellation. 30% of exit survey responses cite "temporary reasons" — they'd stay if they could pause.

### Who is affected?
~2,000 subscribers/month across all plans. Highest concentration in mid-tier plans ($15-30/month) where price sensitivity is moderate but lifetime value is significant.

### What is the impact?
- Lost MRR: ~$40K/month in preventable cancellations
- Re-acquisition cost: 3-5x higher than retention
- NPS impact: Cancellation flow currently scores 2.1/5 in satisfaction

---

## Proposed Solution

### What will we do?
- Add "Pause" as an alternative to "Cancel" in the subscription management flow
- Let store owners configure: max pause duration, max pauses per year, minimum subscription age
- Automated resume with 3-day advance email notification
- Admin dashboard showing paused subscriptions count and projected resume dates

### What will we NOT do (in this version)?
- Partial pause (reduce frequency instead of stopping)
- Automated win-back campaigns during pause period
- Pause analytics dashboard (tracked in existing BI tools instead)

---

## Why Now?

Subscription feature launched in Q4 2025. First cohort of subscribers is now 3+ months old — the period where churn spikes. Implementing pause before churn accelerates gives us the best chance to establish healthy retention baselines. Competitor ReCharge launched pause last quarter; merchants are asking for parity.
